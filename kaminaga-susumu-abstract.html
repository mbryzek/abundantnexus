Kaminaga Susumu Abstract

Trillion Sensors – A Visionary Concept

Susumu Kaminaga SPP Technologies.Co. Ltd.

Launched in 2013, the Trillion Sensors (TSensors) Initiative was a visionary effort alongside the Internet of Things (IoT) to connect a vast number of sensors worldwide to address global challenges and achieve a self-sufficient world: Abundance. Janusz Bryzek, motivated by the concepts of Abundance and his long experience in high growth sensor markets, realized that trillions of sensors would not be possible without a targeted commercialization effort.  Thus was born the TSensors Summit Conference: an effort to provide the focus needed to accelerate MEMS and the sensor market to Abundance.

In the first part of this talk, the speaker will focus on the dramatic growth of Micro Electro Mechanical Systems (MEMS) over the last few decades, and how MEMS plays an essential role in the emerging world of IoT and TSensors.  One of the key technologies enabling this remarkable growth is the Deep Reactive Ion Etching (DRIE) technology based on the Robert Bosch invented Bosch Process developed and commercialized by the initiative of the speaker.

In the second part of this talk, the speaker will take a comprehensive check-in of the TSensors movement a little over a decade after its founding.  To achieve Abundance by 2040, it was envisioned that trillions of sensors would work in concert with other exponential technologies to provide people with food, medicine and healthcare, a clean environment, disaster resilience and clean energy. Are we on track to achieve this vision?
